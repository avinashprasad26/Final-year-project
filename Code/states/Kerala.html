Famous arts and handicrafts from Kerala

Kerala is a state located at southern India. Thiruvananthapuram is the capital city. Kerala is bordered by the states of Karnataka, and Tamil Nadu. Kerala is a popular tourist destination in India, known for its coconut-lined sandy beaches, backwaters, hill stations, Ayurvedic tourism, and tropical greenery.


Onam is the popular festival celebrated in Kerala. As of 2019, the state of Kerala is divided into 14 districts.

Art and Crafts

1: Mural Paintings

1. Murals representing Hindu mythology in Kerala are known as Kerala mural paintings. Mural paintings are often found in abundance in ancient temples and palaces throughout Kerala, India, dating largely from the 9th to 12th century CE, when this type of art was favoured by the royals. Thirunadhikkara Cave Temple (now in Tamil Nadu) and Tiruvanchikulam have the oldest murals in Kerala's indigenous style. The Shiva Temple in Ettumanoor, Mattancherry Palace's Ramayana murals, and Vadakkumnatha kshetram are among Kerala mural art's best.

2: Coconut shell crafts

2. Brass broidered coconut shell craft of Kerala is the practise of making beautifully carved and brass broidered products such as cups, flower vases, snuff boxes, nut bowls, powder boxes, and spoons out of coconut shells by artisans in Kerala, India. Because the shell is extremely hard, this art requires great skill on the part of the artisan. Kerala's main production centres are in the districts of Thiruvananthapuram and Kozhikode. Though the coconut shell craft is practised in Goa, Tamil Nadu, Pondicherry, the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, and West Bengal, the brass broidered variety is only found in Kerala. Many countries around the world practise coconut shell craft, including Cambodia, Thailand, the Philippines, the Maldives, and Sri Lanka.

Weaving mats from the leaves of screw pine plants is a craft practised primarily by women in Kerala. This craft, which is practised by artisans in all districts of Kerala, has been around for over 800 years. The screw pine mats play an important role in Kerala's traditional customs. These mats were offered to important visitors to homes as honoured articles to sit on. Finer variations of these mats were also used as sleeping beds.

4: Kathakali masks

4. Kathakali, one of Kerala's most well-known forms of dance, has a significant historical and cultural significance. Kathakali is known for its distinctive make-up and attire. Kerala Kathakali Masks are nothing more than masks that mimic the faces of Kathakali dancers with the colours and make-up that are applied to the dancers' faces. The Kathakali Masks are sold as souvenirs and as display pieces. Masks made of clay, plaster of Paris, or paper mache make excellent decorative items. These oddities have green bases and are adorned with beards and black hairdos. The elaborate costumes of the Kathakali dance attract not only domestic tourists, but also international visitors. Characters of the masks differ depending on the colour of the face, beard, and headdress. These distinctions are based on the dancers' performances and the roles they play.
